Week 2: Jan 29- Feb 4


Last Updated: Sun Apr 08 8:16:41 PM

Learning Objectives

  • Fit and interpret models with categorical predictors
  • Identify moderating variables
  • Explain the difference between a stratified model and an interaction model
  • Fit and interpret an interaction model

Prepare

  • Read selected sections from PMA5
  • Review the Course Notebook Ch3 (Model Building). Prepare to answer questions posed in the notes and in the daily list below.

Materials

  • PMA5 Ch 7, 8.4-8.7, 9.3
  • [Article on how to prepare a research proposal]
  • [Article on how to control confounding effects by statistical analysis]

Assignments

  • Multiple Regression assignment (Due Wed 2/7)
  • Model building assignment (Due Fri 2/9)
  • Peer Review Model Building report (Due Sun 2/11)
  • Team project Research proposal (Due 2/16)

Multiple Regression assignment:

  • Using the NCbirths data set
    • Model birthweight using mothers age, smoking habit, and number of visits.
    • Calculate and interpret a 95% prediction interval for a 30-year old smoking mother with 16 visits to the doctor during her pregnancy.
    • Does smoking habit affect the relationship between age and birth weight?
  • PMA5: Ex 7.5, 7.6, 7.15, 7.17
  • Knit to Word or PDF and submit through Google Drive by the due date.

Model Building Report:

In a separate RMD document:

  • Build a model to predict the age in which an adolescent starts smoking.
    • Come up with two competing models and compare them on at least two measures of fit.
  • Describe your steps along the way as if you were writing a report.
    • This includes describing any data management along the way.
  • Knit to PDF, use section level headers to organize your report.
  • This will be peer reviewed.
  • Upload your final PDF document to the Model Building folder in our class Google Drive. Name your file with your username. e.g. modeling_startsmoke_rdonatello.pdf

Monday: Model Building

  • Project Team assignments
  • How can we include non-numeric data in a model?
  • What problems can we encounter when using such data?

Wednesday: Moderation & Interactions

  • What are stratified models and subgroup analysis.
  • How does stratification relate to moderation?
  • How do these concepts differ from interactions?

Friday: Model fit & variable selection

  • Measures of model fit.
  • How to choose variables for inclusion in a model
  • Comparing between models, and deciding when enough is enough and just stop tweaking the model!
